Social Media Optimization

Joon Wheeler WEB110 BIG SMO Social media is not just for socialising anymore. It has become almost essential for advertising and promotion. It now serves as a major source of news, and It is a major mobilisation tool for political campaigns, as well. Jayson DeMers described the benefits of social media optimisation (SMO) in a piece he wrote for Forbes magazine way back in 2014 He suggests everything from lower cost, to higher conversion rates, to increased brand reputation and brand loyalty. If you are actively providing content and interacting with you customers and potential customers, it pays to put yourself out there. Target audiences generally appreciate interaction when choosing where to spend their time and money, so it can take up a lot of time if you’re not careful. If you are not used to spending time on social media platforms, the fast pace and high volume of information can be overwhelming, or even slightly addictive. Limiting time online (preferably based on a posting schedule) can make your efforts more focused, and less stressful. Many of us don’t have as much of a problem with that, and studies have shown that social media does not, itself, create stress, but it can definitely waste a lot of time if it’s not structured. All in all, there is much to gain and almost no loss in implementing a SMO strategy.
